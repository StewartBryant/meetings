Agenda 
  • The Internet Protocol (IP) has been used successfully in our times. But can it still support and implement emerging applications, industry verticals, and network infrastructural changes in the future? Some typical emerging applications include, but not limited to, industrial machine-to-machine communications, industrial control and manufacturing, driverless vehicles, and holographic type communications. The infrastructural changes include, for example, convergence and integration of satellite networks with terrestrial networks, ManyNets, and Industrial IoT. 
  • This is the 2nd in its series of New IP Research Side Meetings with the following as the objectives:
  • -        To share findings and observations about the Internet and its protocols regarding its fundamental weakness, incapability and vulnerability by its design. 
  • -        To explore the impacts of future applications to the IP architecture and protocols
  • -        To investigate the implications of infrastructural changes for the IP architecture and protocols
